Our Philosophy

Alignment Therapeutic Massage is based on the belief that body misalignment leads to unnecessary pain and inflammation. Your treatment is designed to address your pain, retrain your muscle memory, own neural pathways and release restrictions

Techniques

Myofascial Release

a technique completed without the use of oil to release both the muscle tissue and the fascia (connective tissue surrounding the muscle) therefore allowing space for the muscle to relax

Dynamic Cupping

an ancient technique based on traditional Chinese medicine where the focus is to release fascia, bring blood flood to the area to promote healing and works well on neurological complaints

Micro Movements

reduce tension, strain, and discomfort from repetitive strain injuries and immobility using professionally designed unobtrusive movements

Therapeutic Massage

manual manipulation of soft tissue with the goal of not just reaching the superficial layers but also the deeper ones

Trigger Point Therapy

the release of tight areas with specific pain referral patterns through the use of static pressure

Hydrotherapy

involves the application of water of varying temperatures for pain relief and health maintenance

Aromatherapy

combines the use of essential oils with massage or as home therapy to enhance health, reduce stress and relieve pain

Home Exercises and Stretches

effective custom routines for home use to strengthen weakened areas and release tension to tight muscles

About

Crystal Tracy successfully completed the Massage Therapy program through Mount Royal College in 1998 and became a Master Herbalist in 2008.

She has been working in the industry full time ever since and over the years has developed a unique style of treatment that is designed for the maximum release of muscular tension for preventative care, rehabilitation, recovery of injuries, athletes and accidents.

Crystal specializes in treatments due to repetitive strain injuries, motor vehicle accidents, chronic pain and injuries caused by sports. She has worked closely with both professional and amateur athletes.

Since moving to Cochrane one of her passions has been treating junior athletes such as hockey players, dancers, soccer enthusiasts and swimmers.
